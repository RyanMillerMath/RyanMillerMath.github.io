Portfolio

As a software developer at a tech start up, I became well-versed in working independently and with a small team to meet strict deadlines no matter what, whether working any amount of extra hours for no extra pay or serving numerous roles in development. This also required me to pick up several new technologies and figure them out, regarding both software and hardware. Some of my contributions included working with Google's Tango and its API, using quaternions to rotate a steering wheel in a virtual reality car, and some very minor dabbling in the Arduino's C++ library for sensors in an automated aeroponics system.

My biggest contribution was a data visualization platform, which took electromagnetic field data from electrical engineering software and visualized it in virtual reality. For this project, I constructed a parser to read the vector field data, which was stored from the software as a CVS file. I then established a voxel engine that created custom-built voxels pointing in the appropriate vector directions to visualize the data.

Two current projects I'm working on are an automated lawnmower and a mobile education app. With the automated lawnmower, I aim to produce the first automated lawnmower that doesn't require any assembly out of the box. My secret recipe: it's also technically bladeless. With the education app, I seek to teach math topics ranging from beginner to advanced since all the current math "education" apps on the market focus almost purely on speed arithmetic and not truly useful knowledge. If the app becomes popular when it's complete, I plan on branching it into other fields, i.e. coding, physics, history, etc., making it a versatile, all-encompassing source of self-education and/or guidance.
